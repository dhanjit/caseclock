Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026

CaseClock is built so that your case data never leaves your device. There is no account, no cloud sync, no server, and no analytics. This policy explains that in full.

In one line: We do not collect, transmit, store, or have access to any of your data. Everything you enter stays encrypted on your own device.

What data CaseClock handles

You enter case information — case labels, FIR/PS references, dates, notes, and any files you attach. All of it is stored only on your device, encrypted at rest with a passphrase that you choose. CaseClock has no user accounts and asks for no personal identifiers.

What we collect

Nothing. CaseClock has no servers and makes no network calls to us. Specifically:

  • No account creation, sign-in, or email collection.
  • No cloud sync or remote backup — your data is never uploaded.
  • No analytics, telemetry, advertising, tracking, or third-party SDKs that phone home.
  • No cookies used to track you.

Where your data lives

Data is stored in the app's private, encrypted storage on your device using AES-256 encryption, unlocked by your passphrase. Because there is no cloud copy, only you can access it — and the trade-off is that there is no remote wipe and no automatic backup. Protect your device, use a strong passphrase, and keep your own encrypted backup file somewhere safe.

Notifications

If you allow notifications, CaseClock schedules deadline alerts locally on your device through the operating system. The content of those alerts is generated on-device; nothing about your cases is sent to us or to any third party to deliver them.

TestFlight beta testing

During the TestFlight beta: Apple, as the platform provider, may collect crash logs and basic usage data under Apple's TestFlight & Privacy policy, and shares aggregate crash/usage information with the developer to improve the app. This is Apple's collection, governed by Apple's terms — CaseClock itself still collects nothing. See also the TestFlight terms.

Children's privacy

CaseClock is a professional tool intended for adult users and is not directed at children.

Changes to this policy
